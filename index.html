"Why didn't you match anesthesia the first time?"

Do not be evasive. Do not overcorrect into self-flagellation. I chose IM specifically because it would provide exactly that evidence — objective evaluations, complex inpatient cases, and ICU experience. My performance in IM is my answer to that question."

"Why anesthesia specifically — and why now?"

Do not use vigilance, last line of defense, or advocacy. Instead: frame the perioperative window as a physiologically compelling intersection of chronic disease, acute stress, and real-time systems management that requires exactly the reasoning you've developed in IM.

"What do you bring that others don't?"

Three genuine differentiators: (1) perioperative AKI research with direct specialty relevance, (2) IM-trained longitudinal physiologic reasoning most anesthesia applicants lack, (3) operational leadership experience at scale that maps to ICU coordination and OR systems.

Step 3 strategy — your specific situation

Step 3 must perform above average — this is not optional

Step 3 will not erase Step 2 CK 237 but it CAN reassure programs that your standardized testing is reliable. A score at or above 240 is the target. Steady daily questions from day one, not a cram in month 14.

Phase 1: Foundation (months 1–6) June–Dec 2025
15–20 UWorld Qs on clinical days, 20–25 on lighter days. Never skip more than 2 consecutive days. Focus: cardiopulm, nephrology, ID, hemodynamics. Integrate with whatever rotation you're on. Do not treat Step 3 as a separate project.
Phase 2: Breadth (months 7–12) Jan–June 2026
Cycle through all systems. Begin CCS (Clinical Case Simulations) practice — 13 CCS cases on Step 3 and most residents underprep them. Track your UWorld percentage by category. Target 60%+ in all categories before scheduling.
Phase 3: Integration (months 13–15) July–Sept 2026
2-week dedicated push: 30 Qs/day, daily CCS cases, biostatistics sweep, pharmacology review. Take UWSA or AMBOSS practice exam to calibrate. Schedule exam. Target: 240+.

Step 3 high-yield — always tested

  • Biostatistics Sensitivity, specificity, PPV, NPV, NNT/NNH. Know the 2×2 table. Every single exam.
  • Preventive medicine Screening guidelines — mammography, colonoscopy, lung, cervical. Age thresholds tested every time.
  • CCS cases Order labs, imaging, AND treatment simultaneously. Time management matters — practice the workflow.
  • Ethics/legal Informed consent, capacity, advance directives, surrogate decision-making. Very high-yield.
  • IM core Sepsis, AKI, ARDS, ACS, HF, PE, GI bleeding, DKA/HHS — master these cold. They appear constantly.
  • Pharmacology Drug mechanisms, classic interactions, overdose presentations. High-yield for Step 3 and anesthesia reapplication.
