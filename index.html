Interview prep
Three questions asked at every anesthesia interview. Practice out loud until natural -- not rehearsed.
Q1 -- "Why didn't you match anesthesia the first time?"
"My application had real strengths -- I interviewed at UCSD, Cedars-Sinai, OHSU, and UC Davis. But I had not yet demonstrated sustained clinical performance in a rigorous environment over time. I chose IM specifically to provide exactly that evidence. My performance in IM is my answer to that question."

Key: honest, forward-looking, names the gap and closes it immediately.
Q2 -- "Why anesthesia -- and why now?"
"Internal medicine gave me a longitudinal view of how chronic disease evolves. That training made me increasingly drawn to the perioperative window -- where those chronic trajectories become acutely consequential. Anesthesia requires real-time simultaneous management of interacting organ systems under time pressure. That is the cognitive environment where I feel most engaged."

Key: no vigilance, no advocacy, no last line of defense. Anesthesia as logical destination of IM reasoning.
Q3 -- "What do you bring that others don't?"
"Three things. First, perioperative AKI research -- I am lead investigator on a study examining surgical AKI risk factors. I understand perioperative physiology at a research level. Second, IM-trained longitudinal physiologic reasoning -- most applicants have not managed the chronic disease progression that creates perioperative risk. I have. Third, operational leadership at scale -- $140K fundraising infrastructure, workflow redesign for 300+ families -- which maps directly to perioperative systems thinking."
